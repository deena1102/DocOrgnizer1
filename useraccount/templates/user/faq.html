Frequently Asked Questions

What is DocOrganizer?

DocOrganizer is a web-based system for official and personal document management. It allows users to request official documents from government entities, and these documents are directly transferred from the relevant ministry to the user, without any intervention from the website's system. Additionally, users can create custom categories to store the documents they need, albeit with a limited number. Users can download, view, or manage documents within their custom and system categories.

Why can't I access my official documents?

To access your official documents, you must be physically present at one of our centers. This is necessary for us to verify your identity and determine the specific group to which you belong.

Why does the user need to physically visit the DocOrganizer center?

Users are required to visit the Doc Organizer center to fully verify their identity through personal presence and ID confirmation. This process qualifies the user to be part of the Trusted Users Group, granting access to their system categories and the "My Information" page. These pages house specific information related to the individual with a particular ID number, and access remains restricted until comprehensive identity verification is successfully completed.

How does DocOrganizer protect the documents uploaded by users?

The system encrypts the user's documents using a unique key associated with each user. This key is derived from the user's password, which is securely hashed. Since the actual password is not known or stored, we achieve a level of security akin to end-to-end encryption.

What are custom categories?

Custom categories are user-created sections designed for the private storage of documents. Each user is allowed to create their own 4 categories, and if they wish to modify any category, they can do so by clicking on the "Update Category" option. This feature ensures users have a personalized and secure way to organize their documents.
